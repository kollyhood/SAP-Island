## 1. Executive Summary

This venture is proposed to build a focused SAP channel and services business around four established revenue streams - License, Implementation, AMC, and CRS / Change Requests. The business is not trying to invent a new market. It is entering a proven operating model that already exists in the SAP ecosystem and scaling it with disciplined execution.

The opportunity is credible because the founders are not starting from zero. Shaktivel already has 3 active leads, including 1 opportunity near closure, and the intent is to convert that deal into the new business within a 6-month window. The first account is expected to become the ladder for referenceability, recurring revenue, and further growth.

## 2. Why This Opportunity Exists

Enterprise SAP customers do not buy only software; they buy delivery confidence. To win the deal, the vendor must show named consultants, project governance, implementation readiness, and post-go-live support. That is why this business needs visible capacity before revenue is fully realized.

What this capital enables:

- a delivery-ready consulting pool that customers can evaluate with confidence
- presales, workshops, solutioning, and mobilization during the bid cycle
- working-capital support while enterprise receivables trail delivery milestones

## 3. Revenue Model

The model is designed to monetize the full customer lifecycle:

- License revenue opens the customer account and creates partner fee income.
- Implementation revenue covers configuration, rollout, integration, migration, training, and go-live support.
- AMC revenue creates recurring cash flow and account stickiness after go-live.
- CRS revenue captures enhancement work, reports, interfaces, workflow changes, and other paid modifications.

**In simple terms:** License opens the door, Implementation funds delivery, AMC stabilizes cash flow, and CRS expands profitability.

## 5. Cost and Capacity Case

To win enterprise SAP work, the business must show a visible 10-member consulting team. A practical delivery pyramid based on prevailing manpower bands supports this requirement.

| Experience band | Illustrative mix | Annual cost per person | Annual cost |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 0-10 years | 4 consultants | Rs. 12 lakh | Rs. 48 lakh |
| 10-20 years | 3 consultants | Rs. 24 lakh | Rs. 72 lakh |
| 20-30 years | 2 consultants | Rs. 36 lakh | Rs. 72 lakh |
| 30-40 years | 1 senior architect | Rs. 48 lakh | Rs. 48 lakh |
| **Total payroll** | **10-member team** |  | **Rs. 2.40 Cr** |

After adding presales, delivery management, certification, alliance development, travel, admin, infrastructure, rental commitments, business promotion, and receivables support, the business is expected to operate at an average run-rate of roughly Rs. 35-45 lakh per month, with temporary peaks rising toward Rs. 70 lakh per month during active bid, mobilization, and receivables-carry periods.

The operating plan is further anchored by identified office premises of approximately 6,000 sq.ft with car and bike parking, with a net monthly rental commitment of about Rs. 2.54 lakh inclusive of GST.

## 6. Why Up to Rs. 70 Lakh per Month Can Be Justified

- customers expect to see a real delivery organization before award, not a brochure and a promise
- implementation teams must be visible and mobilization-ready while the sale is still being evaluated
- collections in enterprise projects typically lag the effort already deployed

Accordingly, Rs. 70 lakh should be viewed as peak cash deployment capacity, not as flat monthly burn from day one.

## 8. Operating Expense Anchors

To address investor visibility on overheads, the operating-cost base is now stated more explicitly rather than bundled into a generic admin bucket.

| Expense anchor | Current grounding | Why it matters |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Office premises | Approx. 6,000 sq.ft identified premises | supports delivery readiness, customer meetings, and team visibility |

This does not change the core thesis of the plan. It simply shows that the overhead assumptions now have identified real-world components, which improves investor confidence in the monthly operating model.

## 9. Investor Monitoring View

The business can be tracked through a small set of operating metrics that matter more than broad-market size claims:

- lead conversion from active pipeline to signed order
- consultant utilization as delivery ramps
- monthly cash burn versus peak deployment periods
- receivables cycle and collection discipline
- share of revenue shifting toward AMC and CRS after first closure

**Milestone view:** Setup and team readiness | First closure | Implementation + AMC | Scale from reference account

> **Bottom line**  
> This is an investable ladder-up business: the market is proven, the revenue streams are established, the team already has customer access, and the first account can become the platform for broader growth. The added rental and promotion disclosures strengthen the operating case without changing the underlying model.
